# Selling this as a service

Quick reference for pitching, pricing, and demoing this product to small businesses.

## Positioning

Not "I'll build you a chatbot" — that's generic and commoditized. Instead: **"I'll build an AI assistant trained on your own FAQs/policies that answers customers on your website and WhatsApp instantly, and hands off to you the moment it's unsure."**

The two things that make this different from a cheap Fiverr FAQ bot:
1. It's grounded in the client's actual content (retrieval-augmented generation) — it won't invent prices, policies, or availability.
2. It captures a lead automatically the moment it can't help, so the business never loses a customer to "the bot didn't know."

## Who to target first

Businesses that get repetitive questions and are currently answering them by hand: clinics/dentists, salons, real estate agents, home-service contractors, small e-commerce stores, local agencies. Anyone with a public FAQ page or a WhatsApp number they answer manually is a warm lead.

## Packages (adjust to your market)

| Tier | What's included | One-time | Monthly |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | Website widget, up to ~20 FAQ items, email lead notifications | $300–600 | $50–100 (hosting + API costs) |
| Standard | Everything above + WhatsApp, branded widget, larger knowledge base | $700–1,500 | $100–250 |
| Pro | Everything above + Instagram DMs, ongoing content updates, monthly report, priority support | $1,500–3,000+ | $250–500 |

Instagram is a good Pro-tier differentiator, not a Starter feature: it needs a one-time Meta App review on your side before it's usable for anyone but test users, so don't promise a live Instagram bot on a same-week timeline the first time you set it up.

The monthly fee covers your hosting + their API usage + you keeping their knowledge base current — frame it that way, not as "subscription for the bot."

## Demo script (5 minutes, on a call or Loom)

1. Run `npm run seed-demo`, open the demo widget, ask it 2–3 realistic questions live.
2. Ask a question it *can't* answer — show the honest "I don't have that, let me connect you" handoff and the lead form. This single moment sells the product better than any feature list, because it shows the bot won't embarrass them by making things up.
3. Open the admin dashboard, show the **Leads** tab — "every one of these is a customer who would've bounced without this."
4. Close with: "I can have this live on your site with your real FAQ this week."

## Outreach message (LinkedIn/cold email — keep under 125 words)

> Hi [Name] — noticed [specific thing, e.g. your site's FAQ page / you answer the same booking questions on WhatsApp all day]. I build AI assistants trained on a business's own FAQs that answer customers instantly on the website and WhatsApp, and hand off to a real person the moment it's unsure — so nothing gets answered wrong and no lead falls through the cracks. Happy to send a 2-minute demo built on [Company]'s actual FAQ page, no charge, no obligation. Worth a look?

Follow up 2–3 times if you don't hear back — most replies come from the follow-up, not the first message.

## Objection: "We already have a chatbot / Zapier / Intercom"

Most of those are either scripted (no real understanding) or priced for teams, not a 3-person local business. This is scoped and priced for exactly the business that's too small for Intercom and too underserved by a $20 Fiverr FAQ script.

## Where to list it

- **Upwork**: search "chatbot," "AI agent," "customer support automation" — proposals should reference the *specific* pain in their job post, not a generic pitch.
- **Fiverr**: gig titled around the outcome ("AI chatbot trained on your business FAQs, website + WhatsApp") rather than the tech stack.
- **LinkedIn**: DM local business owners directly; a working demo link beats any amount of copy.
